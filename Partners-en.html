Partners

Realizing the common goals and tasks to which the partner organizations are directed, namely the reduction of economic, social, educational and cultural differences in society, by providing a high level of research and expertise for local and regional actors in the field of social development as citizens with the same human rights, our involvement is imposed not only temporarily while the project lasts, but to give our contribution to the continuity and sustainability of such projects that lead to the improvement of civil society at all levels (horizontal and vertical).

Our intention also includes equivalence in the methodology and the chosen methods that are designed and agreed upon with the partners based on the professional, practical experience of each of the partners and the real needs of the target group, thus ensuring the successful implementation of all project activities.

In that way, the partnership ensures the strengthening and sustainability of the project, because it creates new bases for creating a model with the possibility of combining the best practices needed at the local and regional level, in a unique way it will be reflected at the global level. The project partnership is structured to ensure that best practices, experience, models and lessons learned can be effectively and efficiently adapted to develop a sustainable approach to project implementation.

Institute of Culture and Art

The main activities of the Institute of Culture and Art are related to the problems in culture and art, the processes aimed at the development and transformation of modern challenges, conditioned by the global process in culture, and aimed at the humanization of relations in culture.

The main activities include:

  1. Programming of scientific - research work

  2. Research work in the context of types of culture and in the sphere of art

  3. Literature and literature

  4. Painting

  5. Theater

  6. Music

  7. Architecture

  8. Sculpture

  9. Movie

  10. Archaeology

The evolution of the scientific-research activity through the mutual connection of science and education in coordination with the subjects in the sphere of culture and art simultaneously determines the goal for the continued existence of our Institute. Achieving significant scientific results through domestic and international cooperation through knowledge exchange, research, training as well as application in joint projects in intercultural development in general.

The target group of our Institute is multidimensional, aimed at: the young population, starting with high school students; the students; domestic and foreign scientific research institutions; experts from various organizational and institutional project teams for the realization of joint projects.

At the regional level, the common challenges that are affecting us in the long term at different levels require us to take a series of strategic and innovative measures that should be aimed exclusively at finding common solutions and overcoming all the obstacles caused by traditional ways of living. Taking into account that the Balkans as a regional area is very complex, this implies that the inclusion of partnership relations only in the Balkans can cause bias in cultural diversity, and our intention is always to have representatives outside of this region as partners. This will also contribute to the objectivity of research related to the subject, goals and tasks of the project

The Kreativ Research Association

The expertise of The Kreativ Research Association is focused on the following fields:

  • Education – promoting education and professional training courses in rural areas and in marginalized communities (including Roma communities) paying particular attention and professional counseling to parents and families.

  • Work with young people (including young Roma) and inclusion; starting from 2016 we initiated research and scientific publications on a volunteer basis focusing on the situation of professional competencies of young people and recognizing their participation in the labor market. We also investigate the representatives of the immigrant social class of Romania within the EU after the liberalization of the labor market.

  • Volunteer work and inclusion: we aim to promote activities between students and schools from secondary schools by involving them in more informal types of education.

According to our goals, our activities deal with and connect to several areas such as:

  • Education and professional training

  • Social work research

  • Community development

  • Human rights

  • Environment

  • Youth work and volunteering

In the field of youth work, our activities are in most cases connected with the other activities of the non-governmental organization of the partners. Our main expert has been working for 15 years on youth projects as a representative of the academic field and a representative of the non-governmental sector with people from the Roma community on several projects.

The Creative Research Association (Kreativ Research Association) develops professional programs for the training of undergraduate teachers in cooperation with the Caraș-Severin Center for didactic staff at universities.

ERASMUS PROGRAME

Erasmus is a European Union programme that supports student and staff mobility across Europe. It was launched in 1987 and has since facilitated the exchange of over 10 million people. The Erasmus Programme provides opportunities for students and staff to study, teach, or work in another EU country, allowing them to gain international experience and broaden their horizons. Through Erasmus, participants can develop their skills, knowledge, and cultural understanding, building valuable personal and professional connections along the way.

There are several different Erasmus programmes available, each with its own focus and criteria for eligibility. These programmes include:

  1. Erasmus+ Higher Education: This programme supports mobility for students and staff in higher education institutions. It aims to enhance the quality and relevance of higher education across Europe and promote intercultural understanding.

  2. Erasmus+ Vocational Education and Training: This programme supports mobility for students and staff in vocational education and training. It aims to increase the quality and relevance of vocational education and training across Europe and promote cooperation between education and the world of work.

  3. Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees: This programme supports joint master degrees offered by consortia of higher education institutions. It aims to promote excellence in higher education and foster cooperation between institutions across Europe and the world.

  4. Erasmus+ Sport: This programme supports collaboration between sports organizations and promotes healthy lifestyles. It aims to increase participation in sport and physical activity and to promote social inclusion and equal opportunities in sport.

Overall, Erasmus programmes offer valuable opportunities for personal and professional growth, as well as contributing to the development of a more inclusive and interconnected Europe.
