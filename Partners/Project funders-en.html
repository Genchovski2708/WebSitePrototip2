ERASMUS PROGRAME

Erasmus is a European Union programme that supports student and staff mobility across Europe. It was launched in 1987 and has since facilitated the exchange of over 10 million people. The Erasmus Programme provides opportunities for students and staff to study, teach, or work in another EU country, allowing them to gain international experience and broaden their horizons. Through Erasmus, participants can develop their skills, knowledge, and cultural understanding, building valuable personal and professional connections along the way.

There are several different Erasmus programmes available, each with its own focus and criteria for eligibility. These programmes include:

  1. Erasmus+ Higher Education: This programme supports mobility for students and staff in higher education institutions. It aims to enhance the quality and relevance of higher education across Europe and promote intercultural understanding.

  2. Erasmus+ Vocational Education and Training: This programme supports mobility for students and staff in vocational education and training. It aims to increase the quality and relevance of vocational education and training across Europe and promote cooperation between education and the world of work.

  3. Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees: This programme supports joint master degrees offered by consortia of higher education institutions. It aims to promote excellence in higher education and foster cooperation between institutions across Europe and the world.

  4. Erasmus+ Sport: This programme supports collaboration between sports organizations and promotes healthy lifestyles. It aims to increase participation in sport and physical activity and to promote social inclusion and equal opportunities in sport.

Overall, Erasmus programmes offer valuable opportunities for personal and professional growth, as well as contributing to the development of a more inclusive and interconnected Europe.
